What is LDU Factorization?

LDU factorization, also known as LU factorization with diagonal scaling, is a mathematical process
that breaks down a square matrix into three matrices: a lower triangular matrix (L), a diagonal matrix (D), and an upper triangular matrix (U).

More Details about LDU Factorization

  • purpose:

    • LDU factorization is a fundamental algorithm in linear algebra that's useful for solving
    linear systems, inverting matrices, and computing determinants.

  • Matrices:

    • In the lower triangular matrix, all elements above the diagonal are zero, and in the upper triangular matrix,
    all elements below the diagonal are zero

  • Order

    • The proper row and/or column orderings or permutations are important for the factorization to work

  • Efficiency

    • LU factorization is efficient, taking about half as many operations as finding the row reduced echelon form.

  • Non-square matrices

    • LU decomposition can also be used for non-square matrices. For example, if you have an m × n matrix M,
    you can write M = LU, where L is an m × m matrix and U is an m × n matrix.
